**Sovereign Computational Economics: A Deterministic, Identity‑Anchored Computational Substrate for Economic Systems**

# **ABSTRACT**  
This paper introduces **Sovereign Computational Economics**, a new computational substrate designed for deterministic, identity‑anchored, invariant‑preserving economic systems. Unlike existing architectures—such as blockchains, CBDCs, event‑sourced databases, and distributed ledger systems—this substrate provides a **formally defined mutation pipeline**, a **constitutional invariant layer**, and a **deterministic state‑transition model** capable of reconstructing economic truth with perfect fidelity.

The architecture is built around a **triple‑binary execution model**, an **organ‑based internal structure**, and a **formal identity‑anchoring mechanism** that binds all economic mutation to human or institutional identity. A deterministic synthetic economic model (Synthyosis), grounded in a **Four‑Account Monetary Doctrine**, separates real monetary truth from synthetic economic truth while preserving conservation and constitutional invariants.

A prototype implementation demonstrates multi‑million‑TPS deterministic execution on standard consumer hardware, validating the feasibility of the substrate. The system achieves **bit‑for‑bit identical replay**, invariant‑preserving mutation, and identity‑anchored state transitions across continuous deposit and withdrawal simulations. These results establish Sovereign Computational Economics as a **new computational category**, distinct from distributed systems, consensus protocols, and traditional financial architectures.

The work provides a formal foundation for sovereign, institutional, and humanitarian digital economic systems, and introduces a reproducible computational model for deterministic economic truth.

# **KEYWORDS**  
Deterministic computation; sovereign substrates; identity‑anchored systems; synthetic economics; constitutional invariants; mutation pipelines; economic state machines.
