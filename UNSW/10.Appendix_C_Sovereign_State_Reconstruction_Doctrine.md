# **C.10 Modular Sovereign Subnetworks (The Spine Architecture)**

National digital infrastructure must accommodate the structural reality that governments, financial institutions, municipalities, and public agencies operate as **distinct administrative domains** with their own mandates, data flows, and operational boundaries. The sovereign computational organism addresses this requirement through a **modular spine architecture**, enabling each institution to operate its own **closed‑loop sovereign subnetwork** while remaining interoperable through the constitutional substrate.

---

## **C.10.1 Institutional Modularity**

Each participating institution—such as a national government, central bank, commercial bank, municipality, licensing authority, land registry, or immigration department—operates a **sovereign module** anchored to the spine. These modules are:

- isolated  
- deterministic  
- invariant‑preserving  
- constitutionally bounded  
- interoperable through the Hypernet  

This ensures that institutional autonomy is preserved while enabling cross‑agency coordination.

---

Each module operates as a **closed‑loop network**, ensuring data integrity, fraud resistance, and deterministic interoperability with other agencies.
